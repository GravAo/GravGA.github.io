Research Projects

2024
Common Envelope Phase May Save the Cloud from Depletion
Showed that the common envelope phase during the binaries' evolution history may bring the orbit to rapidly pass certain depletion channels caused by level transitions of the cloud. Enlarged the parameter space where the clouds are still present to the late inspiral stage within the frequency band of LISA.
2024
Mass Transfer of Superradiant Clouds
Modeled how the cloud is resonantly transferred to the companion star with an analytical formalism to calculate the transfer efficiency and resonant radii. This work enables cloud-hosting for non-superradiant objects, such as neutron stars, creating a promising way to probe ultra-light bosons for multi-messenger observation.
2025
Formation and Evolution of the Common Envelope
Modeled the formation of the common envelope from circum-single clouds as binaries inspiral into the cloud, with a gravitational molecule framework applicable to general mass-ratios binaries. The back-reaction of the evolving common envelope can pump up the orbital eccentricity to O(0.1) within the sensitivity band of LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA.
2022
Conversion from Gravitons to Photons
Calculated the conversion rate to photons when the gravitational waves pass through the strong electromagnetic field of a neutron star. Advisor: Prof. Miao Li (Sun Yat-Sen University).
2023
Response of Regular Black Holes to Tidal Perturbations
Undergraduate Thesis: Investigated the response of several regular black holes (e.g., Hayward, Bardeen and Simpson-Visser metrics) to scalar and vector tidal perturbations.
